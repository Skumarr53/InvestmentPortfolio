2. Allocation Rationale: The 4 Engines

Engine 1: Direct Equity (India) - 40%

What: Concentrated portfolio of 10-12 stocks.

Why (Fisher's 15 Points): Mutual funds are forced to diversify (often owning 50-100 stocks). This "di-worsification" kills alpha. By picking 10 "Outstanding" companies with massive R&D and integrity, you capture the full growth curve.

How: We will screen for companies with >20% Revenue growth and <0.5 Debt/Equity.

Engine 2: Global Tech (US) - 30%

What: Nasdaq 100 (MON100) or S&P 500 (MASP500).

Why: The INR has historically depreciated ~3-4% annually against the USD. Investing in US tech gives you Currency Alpha + Innovation Alpha. You own the world's AI infrastructure (Nvidia, Microsoft) which Indian MFs cannot access effectively.

How: Use Motilal Oswal or Navi US Index funds for low-cost entry.

Engine 3: Aggressive MFs (Momentum) - 20%

What: Quant Small Cap + HSBC Midcap.

Why: These funds use momentum algorithms (VLRT) to churn portfolios fast. They are your "Tactical" engine that rides market waves. We keep this at 20% to limit "Algorithm Risk."

How: Continue SIPs; rebalance if they grow >25% of total portfolio.

Engine 4: Speculative Alpha - 10%

What: Crypto (BTC/ETH) or High-Growth Smallcases.

Why: In an "Aggressive Machine," you need a moonshot. BTC/ETH act as "Digital Gold" and a hedge against global monetary expansion.

How: Limit to 10%. If it doubles, sell the principal and ride the "house money."

3. The 100% Small/Mid Cap Risk

You asked if I am okay with 100% Small/Mid cap allocation in your MF bucket. Honestly? No.

The "Machine" Correction

Small/Mid caps have high "Beta" (they move 1.5x the market). In a 10% Nifty correction, your MF portfolio will drop 15-20%. To build a true machine, we need a Defensive Buffer. I recommend moving 10% of this bucket into a Nifty 50 Index to act as a shock absorber.
