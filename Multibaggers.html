What it actually does

ALAB sells retimers, smart cable modules, and CXL fabric switches — the high-speed signal-integrity silicon that lets GPUs, CPUs, and memory pools talk to each other at 64–128 GT/s without packet loss. As rack-scale AI systems (NVL72-class) replace single-server boxes, every PCIe Gen5/6 lane between racks needs ALAB silicon.

Why this is a multibagger (not just a winner)

Three compounding edges: (1) Standard ownership — ALAB co-chairs the CXL consortium, so the spec moves at their pace. (2) Hyperscaler design-in — once a retimer is in a reference design, switching costs are 18–24 months of validation. (3) Margin asymmetry — they sell silicon at software-like 70% GM because the value is in the IP, not the wafer.

The multibagger math

If rack-scale GPU systems hit 10M units/yr by 2029 (vs ~1M today) and ALAB averages $400 silicon per rack at 70% GM → $4B revenue, $1.6B FCF. At 30x FCF (still cheaper than NVDA) = $48B market cap. Current cap: ~$15B → ~3.2x, before optionality on CXL memory pooling.

Red flags & triggers

CUSTOMER CONC. Top 3 customers = ~70% revenue. UCIe COMPETITION Open standard could erode CXL moat post-2028. WATCH NVDA's Quantum-X800 attach rate — if ALAB is in it, conviction → 95.

The multibagger math
